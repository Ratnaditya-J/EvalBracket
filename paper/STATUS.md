# Paper status

The current arXiv draft reports the v0.3 through v2.2 control and proxy-task experiments. It predates
the launch-facing v3 input and evidence architecture implemented in July 2026.

In particular, the current paper does not yet describe:

- mandatory provider-defined capability frameworks and harm thresholds;
- the frozen evaluation-result-to-level mapping manifest;
- manual red-team campaigns as first-class evidence inputs;
- aggregation across manual, automated, external, tool, agent, and adaptation evidence;
- level-specific qualification under arbitrary provider scales;
- the public, restricted, and internal disclosure layers.

The existing empirical results remain valid within their stated proxy-task scope. They must not be
presented as provider-defined cyber or biological system-card claims. A later paper revision should
describe v3 as the application and reporting layer while retaining v2.2 as engineering evidence.
